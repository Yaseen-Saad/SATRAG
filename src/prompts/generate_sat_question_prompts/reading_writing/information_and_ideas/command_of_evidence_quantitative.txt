- "Command of Evidence — Quantitative": The passage includes a table, bar graph, line graph, or scatterplot. The student must interpret the data to answer the question. The data should be clear and readable. Easy: read a value directly from a bar graph or table. Medium: compare two data points or calculate a simple difference. Hard: identify a trend, interpret a rate of change, or determine which data point best supports or contradicts a claim.
- When including data, describe it clearly in the passage_text field using simple HTML tables or structured text. The data must be complete enough to answer the question without external context.
- Avoid data that requires complex calculations — the SAT tests interpretation, not arithmetic.
