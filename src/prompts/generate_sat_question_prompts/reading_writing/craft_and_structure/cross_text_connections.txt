- "Cross-Text Connections": Two short passages (70-120 words each) on the same topic (e.g., two scientists discussing a phenomenon, two historical documents, two reviews of the same book). The student must identify how the authors' perspectives relate — agreement, disagreement, shared assumption, or complementary viewpoints. IMPORTANT: Each passage must be 70-150 words long. The passages should present DIFFERENT perspectives or aspects of the same topic. The question asks about the relationship between the texts, NOT about a single text. Easy: one author clearly agrees/disagrees with the other on a stated point. Medium: authors share a topic but emphasize different aspects or reach different conclusions. Hard: the relationship is implicit — authors may agree on facts but disagree on implications, or one author may address a concern the other raises indirectly.
